THE LIGHTHOUSE OF ALEXANDRIA

HISTORY

The Lighthouse of Alexandria, also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, was a towering structure located on the island of Pharos in Alexandria, Egypt. It was built in the 3rd century BC during the reign of Ptolemy I.

The lighthouse was approximately 120 meters tall and was considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It served as a navigational aid for ships entering the port of Alexandria, guiding them safely to shore.

The structure was built with white marble and featured three sections: a square base, a middle octagonal section, and a cylindrical top. The top of the lighthouse housed a large mirror that reflected sunlight during the day, and a fire was lit at night to provide illumination.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria stood for centuries as a symbol of the city's wealth and prosperity. However, it was severely damaged by several earthquakes over the years and eventually collapsed in the 14th century AD.

WHAT TO SEE

Although the Lighthouse of Alexandria no longer exists, you can still explore the rich history and cultural heritage of Alexandria. Some of the key highlights include:

  • Qaitbay Citadel: Visit the Qaitbay Citadel, a 15th-century fortress built on the site of the original lighthouse. It offers panoramic views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Alexandria National Museum: Explore the museum, which houses a collection of artifacts from ancient Egypt, including objects related to the lighthouse.
  • Alexandria Corniche: Take a stroll along the waterfront promenade known as the Corniche and enjoy the views of the Mediterranean Sea.
