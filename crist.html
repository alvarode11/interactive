THE CRIST THE REDEMEER

HISTORY

The Cristo Redentor, also known as the Christ the Redeemer, is a statue of Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It was built in the 20th century as a symbol of Brazilian Christianity.

The statue stands at approximately 30 meters tall, excluding its pedestal, and overlooks the city of Rio de Janeiro from the Corcovado mountain. It is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone and has become an iconic landmark of Brazil.

The construction of the Cristo Redentor started in 1922 and was completed in 1931. The statue represents Jesus Christ with open arms, symbolizing peace and welcoming visitors to the city.

Today, the Cristo Redentor is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Brazil, attracting millions of visitors each year.

WHAT TO SEE

In addition to the Cristo Redentor statue itself, there are several other attractions to explore in Rio de Janeiro:

  • Sugarloaf Mountain: Take a cable car ride to the top of Sugarloaf Mountain and enjoy panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro.
  • Copacabana Beach: Relax on the famous Copacabana Beach and soak up the sun.
  • Ipanema Beach: Visit Ipanema Beach, known for its beautiful scenery and vibrant atmosphere.
