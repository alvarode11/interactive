THE HANGING GARDERN OF BABYLON

HISTORY

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and are believed to have been located in the ancient city of Babylon, in present-day Iraq. These gardens were built by King Nebuchadnezzar II in the 6th century BC, as a gift for his wife Amytis.

According to ancient accounts, the gardens were an engineering marvel, consisting of terraced gardens with lush vegetation, trees, and flowing water. They were designed to resemble a mountain landscape and were supported by stone columns and arches.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon are renowned for their beauty and were considered a symbol of wealth and luxury in the ancient world. However, their exact location and existence have been a subject of debate among historians.

WHAT TO SEE

Although the Hanging Gardens of Babylon no longer exist, you can still explore their legacy and learn about their historical significance. Some of the key highlights include:

  • Ancient Babylon Ruins: Visit the archaeological site of ancient Babylon and see the remnants of this once-great city.
  • Babylonian Artifacts: Explore museums that display artifacts from Babylon, including reliefs, sculptures, and inscriptions.
  • Historical Documentation: Study ancient texts and accounts that describe the grandeur of the Hanging Gardens.
