About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

History

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 and is considered one of the greatest architectural masterpieces in the world. It attracts millions of visitors every year and is a symbol of love and romance.
