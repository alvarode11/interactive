CHICHÉN ITZÁ

HISTORY

Chichén Itzá is an ancient Mayan city located in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico. It was one of the largest and most important Mayan cities, thriving between the 9th and 12th centuries AD. The city played a significant role in Mayan civilization, serving as a major political, economic, and religious center.

Chichén Itzá is known for its impressive architectural structures, including the iconic El Castillo pyramid, also known as the Temple of Kukulcan. This pyramid stands as a testament to the advanced knowledge and mathematical precision of the Mayans.

In 1988, Chichén Itzá was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and in 2007 it was recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

WHAT TO SEE

Chichén Itzá features several remarkable structures and points of interest:

  • El Castillo (Temple of Kukulcan): The iconic step pyramid that served as a temple and astronomical observatory.
  • The Great Ballcourt: The largest ball court in Mesoamerica, where the Mayans played their ceremonial ball game.
  • The Temple of the Warriors: A large temple complex adorned with intricate stone carvings and columns.
  • The Sacred Cenote: A natural sinkhole that was used for sacrificial offerings.
  • The Observatory: A cylindrical tower used for astronomical observations.
